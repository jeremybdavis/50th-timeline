Improving life. Making history.

Fifty years ago, HCA’s founders – Dr. Thomas Frist Sr., Dr. Thomas Frist Jr. and Jack C. Massey – firmly planted our culture in a commitment to provide superior healthcare with warmth and compassion for patients, colleagues and communities.

As we celebrate our 50th year as a company, our mission remains steadfast and guides our future:

Above all else, we are committed to the care and improvement of human life.

“The great hospitals will always put the patient and the patient’s family first, and the really great institutions will provide care with warmth, compassion, and dignity for the individual.”

– Dr. Thomas Frist Jr. HCA Co-Founder

50 Years of Compassionate Care

1968

HCA Founded

HCA is founded by two physicians, Drs. Thomas Frist Sr. and Thomas Frist Jr., and businessman Jack C. Massey, introducing a new model for hospital care in the United States.

1971

Early Growth

By the end of 1971, HCA operates 40 hospitals housing 5,250 beds in 12 states. Press and investors declare that HCA has become the dominant force in the nation's proprietary hospital business.

1971

Frist Humanitarian Award

The Frist Humanitarian Award is created to honor individuals within HCA who demonstrate a level of commitment and caring that goes beyond everyday acts of kindness, and who inspire colleagues with their compassion, dedication and spirit.

1975

International Ventures

HCA takes on its first international venture with a hospital management contract in Saudi Arabia.

1978

Celebrating 10 Years

By the end of its 10th year, HCA operates more than 120 facilities in 25 states and four foreign countries.

1982

HCA Foundation

The HCA Foundation is created to fund philanthropic contributions to medical education, the arts and community service.

1985

The First Data Center

HCA builds a state-of-the-art data center, linking individual hospital systems to improve operating efficiencies and allowing the company to build software to meet the changing information requirements of each hospital.

1989

Back to the Basics

Dr. Frist Jr. takes HCA private, leading a $5.1 billion leveraged buyout. As a private company, HCA rebuilds its strength. HCA stock will once again be traded publically in 1992.

1993-1995

Growth and Acquisitions

HCA grows rapidly after the 1994 megamerger with Columbia Hospital Corporation. In 1995, Columbia acquires HCA’s spin-off hospital company, HealthTrust, which operates 119 hospitals. By the end of that year, the Columbia/HCA network grows to more than 340 hospitals, 135 surgery centers and 550 home health locations. The rapid succession of mergers and the return of HealthTrust will later form the basis of HCA’s operations.

1997

Improving Patient Safety

Dr. Frank Houser, HCA's former medical director and senior vice president of quality, begins building the company's Quality Review System (QRS) to measure and report performance of all of HCA's hospitals.

1999

LifePoint and Triad Spinoffs

HCA receives approval for two spinoff hospital companies, LifePoint and Triad. Both companies successfully tailor their strategies and operations to the defining characteristics of their hospitals and communities.

1999

HealthTrust

HCA uses its size advantage to create one of the industry’s largest group purchasing organizations. Borrowing from an old name, the GPO becomes HealthTrust Purchasing Group. By its 10th anniversary, HealthTrust will have almost 4,000 members and an annual purchasing power of $17 billion. HealthTrust’s supplier diversity program is recognized by many as the most successful in the healthcare industry.

2004

Sarah Cannon

HCA and Tennessee Oncology PLLC create the Sarah Cannon Research Institute, leveraging the research of 500 HCA physicians. The institute is named for Sarah Cannon, a television and radio personality known worldwide for her Grand Ole Opry character “Minnie Pearl,” who was treated for breast cancer at the founding institute in Nashville. In its first year, the institute will launch 120 clinical trials.

2005

The Hope Fund

Following strong Florida hurricanes in 2004, HCA employees seek to help struggling colleagues. The Hope Fund is formed to provide financial aid and resources to help employees rebuild after an extended illness, injury, disaster or other special situations. By 2018, the Hope Fund will help more than 23,000 families. (Visit Website)

2005

Caring for Tiny Patients

HCA becomes the first healthcare company to mandate a simple $1 test to save babies from a severe form of brain damage caused by excessive jaundice. The same year, HCA enhances infant safety by creating bar-coded tags for babies’ ankles to ensure they receive the proper medical care and that each baby goes home with the right family.

2006

Record-Breaking LBO

The company again becomes privately owned in a $33 billion leveraged buyout – the largest buyout in Wall Street history at the time. HCA will offer another public offering of its stock in 2011.

2007

Leading the Way

HCA and other investor-owned hospital leaders put their minds to work on resolving the issue of the uninsured and launching the Health Coverage Passport plan. Among other strategic amendments, the plan’s multipronged approach proposes affordable health coverage for all Americans. Many of the proposals were later included in the government's health care law.

2010

Innovators Award

HCA launches the HCA Innovators Award to recognize innovative thinkers whose ideas improve patient quality, service or the efficiency of operations.

2011

Parallon

Officially branding their shared service model, HCA creates Parallon Business Solutions, a subsidiary that offers healthcare providers a complete range of industry-leading business services. Parallon leverages HCA’s best business practices and collective experience, scale and expertise for customers in the healthcare industry.

2014

HCA Leadership Institute

HCA Chairman and CEO Milton Johnson announces the launch of the HCA Leadership Institute. The institute advances the company’s vision by building strategic and operational capabilities internally, rapidly deploying best practices and cultivating leaders who exemplify HCA values.

2014-2016

Wired for the Future

HCA Information Technology and Services (IT&S) builds the largest clinical data management system in the healthcare industry. With 20 million patient encounters in 2015 alone, HCA utilizes big data to improve quality of care, reduce costs and – most importantly – save lives. Through foundational technology like a clinical data warehouse and integrated clinical information systems and technologies – including iMobile and PatientKeeper – HCA facilities are an integrated healthcare system both locally and nationwide.

2017-2018

Serving More Communities

HCA enhances its ability to improve medical care and patient access in communities by engaging in several strategic acquisitions. The acquisitions come from Dallas-based Tenet Healthcare and Franklin, Tenn.-based Community Health Systems. In early 2018, HCA entered the Savannah market – the company’s first new market since 2003 – with the purchase of Memorial Health.

2018

HCA's 50th Anniversary

“HCA’s shared values of honesty, integrity, and respect for all people unite us and are vital to our mission to care for and improve human life.”

– Milton Johnson Chairman and Chief Executive Officer

50 Years of Strengthening the Human Spirit

HCA Co-Founder Dr. Thomas Frist Sr. once said, “Good people beget good people,” and that belief continues to prevail today. At HCA, we are committed to providing culturally competent care and fostering a culture of inclusion that embraces and nurtures our patients, colleagues, partners, physicians and communities. Through care, leadership and support of one another, we are strengthening our communities and making lasting, positive change.

“We appreciate our colleagues across the country, whose dedication to evidence-based clinical practices helps ensure the best possible outcomes for the patients we’re privileged to treat.”

- Dr. Jonathan Perlin President, Clinical Services and Chief Medical Officer

50 Years of Clinical Breakthroughs

At HCA, we are driven to help clinicians discover and deliver predictive, precise, and personalized medicine for their patients. In 2013, HCA’s comprehensive infection prevention study, (REDUCE) MRSA, showed a 44 percent decrease in bloodstream infections in ICU patients. Another example of groundbreaking clinical research, which set the standard for care, is when HCA implemented a special screening of newborns to prevent brain damage (kernicterus) caused by severe jaundice.

“Innovation at HCA takes many forms, yet the common denominator is a creative approach that challenges the status quo.”

– Sam Hazen President and Chief Operating Officer

50 Years of Cutting Edge Innovation

HCA is constantly inventing and developing systems to advance health and patient care. From making life-changing discoveries at Sarah Cannon – the cancer institute of HCA – to partnering with the CDC and Harvard Pilgrim Institute to championing healthy pregnancies with the March of Dimes, HCA continues to invest in innovations that strengthen hospitals, improve the practice of medicine, and advance national standards of patient-centered healthcare.

“There is so much good to do in the world and so many different ways to do it.”

– Dr. Thomas Frist, Sr. HCA Co-Founder

50 Years of Community Engagement

In 2016, our employees pledged $23.2 million in cash donations and thousands of volunteer hours to charitable community organizations. Our model of giving – led by leadership, service, and financial support of our employees in their communities – reflects our view that a strategic outlay of talent, resources, and time yields stronger communities.

As we celebrate our 50th anniversary and look toward the next 50 years, we will continue to cultivate compassionate and patient-centered care, advance creativity and innovation, assist clinicians in delivering precise and personalized medicine, and strengthen the communities we serve.
